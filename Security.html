Home Lock Safety

Home Security Tips & Hardening

Implement these simple physical security practices to reduce residential vulnerability and prevent forced entry.

Reinforce Door Frames

Install heavy-duty strike plates with 3-inch screws that anchor directly into the wall studs, preventing kick-in attempts.

Cylinder Pin Upgrades

Upgrade standard deadbolts to cylinders with spool or serrated pins, making lockpicking and bumping extremely difficult.

Pathway Security Lighting

Install motion-activated floodlights around dark corridors and main exit points to eliminate blind zones for burglars.

Lock Tutorials

Smart Lock Configuration Guides

Step-by-step masterclasses on configuring, updating, and troubleshooting next-generation biometric and keypad locks.

Enrolling Biometric Fingerprints

Biometric scanning relies on high-resolution capacitive sensors to read skin ridges. Follow these steps to register templates securely without failure.

1 Wipe the scanner lens clean with a microfiber cloth to clear old scan grease.
2 Access the programming menu using your master password code.
3 Press your finger firmly at multiple angles to register the outer margins.
4 Verify enrollment by running 3 test entry trials before closing the entryway.

Updating Keypad Access Pin Codes

Regular password code rotation is critical. Learn the correct key command sequence to assign new custom entry pins.

1 Press the program button located inside the interior battery casing.
2 Enter the existing Master Pin code, followed by the star (*) key.
3 Input the new user Pin (4-8 digits) and press the pound (#) symbol.
4 Test the code from the outside while keeping the door wide open.

Emergency USB Power Jump-Starts

If your smart lock batteries drain completely, you can utilize external emergency battery contacts to unlock the cylinder.

1 Locate the 9-volt battery contacts or micro-USB port on the bottom of the lock face.
2 Hold a fresh 9V battery against the contacts or connect a portable power bank.
3 Wait for the keypad display screen to light up and enter your valid Pin code.
4 Replace the internal AA alkaline batteries immediately once inside.

Lock Maintenance Checklist

FAQ Help

Security & Lock FAQ

Quick answers to help you understand safety standards, master keys, smart cylinders, and security updates.

What is the difference between rekeying and replacing a lock?

Rekeying retains the existing lock housing but replaces the internal pins to fit a new key layout, invalidating previous keys. Replacing swap-out the entire physical lock and knob assembly with new hardware, which is recommended if the lock body is damaged or worn.

What lock brands provide the highest level of bump resistance?

Brands that incorporate high-security cylinders (like Medeco, Schlage Primus, and Mul-T-Lock) utilize patented sidebars, rotating pin chambers, and telescoping pin designs that make key bumping attempts mechanically impossible.

How often should smart lock batteries be replaced?

Most premium smart locks require battery replacements every 9 to 12 months under standard usage (approx. 10 cycles per day). We recommend replacing them immediately when the low battery indicator turns on or the system health score dips.
